Research

For websites to be visually beautiful and engaging, inspiration is essential. In our effort to build a remarkable webpage, we perused a variety of online resources and took inspiration from the originality and innovation displayed by other websites. Whether it was the slick minimalism, brilliant color schemes, or fascinating interactive aspects, every website we came across had its own special appeal. we carefully scrutinized and absorbed the spirit of the design elements on anything from blogs to professional portfolios, e-commerce websites. We learned what makes a website visually beautiful and user-friendly by examining the structure, typography, layout, and overall user experience. We started using similar design concepts and principles on our own website after becoming inspired by them.



Home Page

For the design of our user-friendly and aesthetically pleasing interface for our homepage, we looked to the well-known e-commerce site AliExpress. On the main display, one of the items we added was an image slider that gave customers the option to scroll through a number of eye-catching pictures of the featured goods. Furthermore, we included product display cards that highlighted important details such product titles, pricing, and photos. These cards streamline the purchasing process by giving customers the ease of a "Add to Cart" button right on the card while also acting as an alluring preview. A "Show More" button that takes customers to a specific product display page, where they may browse a greater selection of products across several categories, was added to further improve the user experience.

Product Page

To give our users a convenient and attractive buying experience, we took design cues from the "The Camera Store" website. Utilizing display cards to highlight our items was one of the design components we used. In addition to stunning product photographs, these product cards also include crucial details like pricing and a "Add to Cart" button, which makes it simple for customers to decide whether or not to make a purchase. We also got ideas from where the cart option is placed on the "The Camera Store" website, making sure that it is clearly visible and easy to find so that visitors can simply manage their chosen things. We added a drop-down menu to the site to improve categorization and navigation, allowing customers to quickly browse through several subcategories including lenses, cameras, and accessories.

In addition to the style cues from "The Camera Store," we concentrated on developing an interesting and instructive product display page. Users can browse a broad variety of products in numerous categories on this page, which has been designated as such. A thorough explanation of each product is provided, along with top-notch photos, a description, specifications, and user feedback. Directly on the product display cards, we included a "Add to Cart" button and clear pricing information to aid in decision-making. We want to give users the knowledge they need to make smart purchasing decisions and create a joyful shopping experience by offering thorough and aesthetically pleasing product presentations.

Blog Page

To construct a user-friendly platform for displaying blogs, we took design ideas from "Under Armour" and "Help Scout". The original blog listing page borrowed design influences from "Under Armour," with a contemporary layout that showcases highlighted blogs with eye-catching images, punchy headlines, and succinct summaries. Visitors should feel at ease and encouraged to explore further with this design. We utilized "Help Scout's" straightforward and user-friendly style for the individual blog display page. We used well-structured font, lots of white space, and a clutter-free layout to prioritize reading and legibility. A seamless and delightful reading experience was what we set out to achieve. Our goal was to create a visually beautiful platform that effectively displays blogs while maintaining user pleasure with an intuitive design by fusing components from both websites.

About Us Page

We looked to the website for "Ada" for design ideas so that we could present our team in a visually beautiful and enticing way on our "About Us" page. We used Ada's design as a model to develop a layout that emphasizes the special skills and qualifications of each team member.
